Kana preface

Writen by KinoTsurayuki

The
seeds in
peoples' minds grow
into tens of thousands of poetry.
People find their paths for sensations
through whatever catches their eyes and ears.

やまと歌は、人の心を種として、万の言の葉とぞなれりける
世の中にある人、ことわざ繁きものなれば、
心に思ふことを、見るもの
聞くものにつけて
言ひ出せる
なり

When we
listen to nightingales
in the blossoms or frogs croak
at the pond, we may wonder, “Which of
a1l the creatures of this world does not sing?"

花に鳴く鶯 水にすむ蛙の声を聞けば
生きとし生けるもの、いづれか
歌を詠まざりける

They are songs
that without effort
shake heaven and earth
move divine spirits, soothe feelings
between people, and ease a warrior's violent soul

力をも入れずして天地をうごかし、目に見えぬ鬼神をも
あはれと思はせ、男女の仲をも和らげ、
猛きもののふの心をも
なぐさむるは
歌なり

I translated the original, referring Toyo University, A. C. Yu , and Poetic Spirit: Lyricism of Kokinshu.
