Hexagon UX is to support womxn & non-binary folx in UX.

  • RoleCommunity organizer, Researcher
  • Date2019-present
  • SiteHexagon UX
  • TalkIxDD 2020
Like most design organizations, Hexagon UX typically hosts events, and we were no different. However, after hosting events in Tokyo for a year and a half, we noticed that many designers often mentioned casually that they felt lost in their career path, not sure how to connect into the community or even unsure if they were actually a designer. We wondered if there might be a way for us to write these thoughts down for the community. Motivated to better understand the community we were serving, we started a research project to ask designers in Japan about their personal stories of resilience.
For this research project, we used the following methods.
  • SNSs to reach reserach target
  • Google forms to collect data
  • various communication tools for 5 days daily promt conversation with the interviewee requests
  • Miro for synthesis
  • Google Meet for depth interview
  • Google Docs to work to draft the report

Simeji is IME to help user communicate more uniquely

  • Role Designer
  • Date2013-2016
  • Sitesimeji.me
  • AwardApple Best of 2014 Japan
Simeji is IME that provides unique communciation ways for the users. For example, it provides cloud base text prediction which dictionary updated over the time based on the user trends. It was initially developped for Android which had over 14 million users by 2014. And in 2014, Responding to that iOS 8 accepts the 3rd party IME for iOS, we ddeveloped the app for iOS in 3 months to release. At the following year, we updated its android design to adapt material design.
With design analysis, we used the following methods for the design decisions.
  • AB testing
  • Cross section online form data analysis
  • Focus group interview
  • User interview
  • User behaviour data analysis

When we adapted the material design, we analyzed the design issues in the application. One of the biggest issues we tried to solve was main input key character legibility.
