Ways to say

  • RoleDesigner, Programmer
  • GitHub YukinoKoh/ways2say
Imagination to me is exploring the possibility based on certain assumption or hypothesis of which whether we are aware or not. Working with people who have over 10 different nationalities, I started to assume that advising on language would help people. I noticed sometimes people say something offensive to another without knowing they could say the same things in a nicer way. It seems to happend many places these days as Erin Meyer wrote in her book, The Culture Map.This app is for such users to be aware their language and suggests alternative ways to say. This chatbot gives the user feedback on what the user is about to say.
Another assumption I had was that it is beneficial to use Facebook developer built in NLP, because the number of cases collected to judge intention behind the language is larger than I can collect individually, and because I guess this ML classification training results in the API to dismantle the language into reasonable sentimental entity.
In actual, I found people like the simplicity of this app desgin. The chatbot has actually a few suggestion patterns per each sentiment or langauge group. For example if the language include 'denial' chat bot returns one of 6 sentences I have set, including 'It may oversight about ---', 'It may assumed too much about ---.', and 'Let's talk about it again.' User liked the instance response to what they said with expected responses. So far, I heard from the users that it is interesting to use, and received 'like's from people I've never met.
Chatbot seems a familiar interface for many users. Although what it does as a system is same whether it is a box form or chatbot. Both are input data and returned data. The power here I imagine is the intuitive impresion that the user receives.

Processing with type

  • RoleFont designer, Programmer
  • Site /processing-type/
  • GitHub /YukinoKoh/processing-type
Graduated from the art school with printing major, I was curious how new media, digital will affect our aesthetics. Like Letterpress printing changed the way for us to inherit knowledge in the 15th century in Europe, and woodprinting did so in the 8th century in Japan, internet has been changing our way to find knowledge. Like many designers have been fascinated with the editorial design which is a way to give a shape to the thoughts, I'm curious how this onlin media affects on our way to digest knowledge and how it would affect our perception. Accordingly, I had assumption before this project that digitally generated view affects on our fundamental aesthetics and perception. I started this type drawing experiment with Processing to image how our perception and the role of designers/artist may change in the future.
During this project, I tried to translate my letter observation and aesthertics into the instructions, lines of codes. I thought the languages I use for printing and coding were somewhat very similar, because it was important for both media that we plan work concept and information hierarchy outside the execution process.
In 1970s, Sol LeWitt explored this concept by giving instruction to the machine to execute his artwork. He said 'All of the planning and decisions are made beforehand and the execution is a perfunctory affair.' In another word, anyone can execute his artwork based on the instruction.
